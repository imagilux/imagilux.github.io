## Why we don't publish a fixed rate card

Light Out adoption depends on factors that vary widely between operators: fleet size, regulatory posture, air-gap requirements, custom integrations, support tier. A flat per-node rate would either be unfair to small teams or under-priced for hyperscale. Instead, we quote against the actual shape of each deployment.

## What's typically in scope

- **UMF tooling** — the open specification is free to use; commercial support, signed image registries, and operator licensing are paid.
- **BOREAL** — node-scaled licensing with volume tiers, plus enterprise add-ons (air-gap, FedRAMP support, dedicated TAM).
- **Professional services** — onboarding, migration, custom operator development.

## How a quote conversation usually goes

1. **Discovery call** (30 min) — fleet size, target environments, current pain points.
2. **Technical fit review** — we sanity-check that Light Out is the right answer for what you're trying to do. Sometimes it isn't, and we'll say so.
3. **Quote** — fleet-scaled commercial terms with explicit support, SLA, and renewal clauses. No surprise overage line items.
4. **Proof of value window** — we deploy a representative slice before you sign anything bigger than a starter contract.

## Open-source vs commercial

The UMF specification, the SDKs, and most reference implementations are open and remain so — see the [Platform](/platform/) overview. Commercial licensing pays for hardened distributions, signed registries, support contracts, and the BOREAL control plane.
